The Artist

Mudbone is a Nashville-based singer, songwriter, guitarist, and producer whose debut double album Rivers & Roads broke into the Top 100 on the Americana charts independently — twice. Gifted his blues name in a chance encounter with a protégé of blues pioneer Son House, he has spent 25 years building a reputation as one of the most compelling and versatile live performers in American roots music.

He grew up in Arkansas in a gospel and bluegrass family, began traveling to the Mississippi Delta at age 12, and was performing professionally by 13 — filling in on New Year's Eve for a departing guitarist in front of 300 people and earning his first two hundred-dollar bills that night. At 20 he took the Nashville exit off I-55 and never looked back.

Today he performs 250+ shows a year in configurations ranging from solo acoustic to full band productions of up to 9 pieces. His music blends blues, soul, country, folk, and rock and roll into what he calls "a musical gumbo of American roots music" — a sound broad enough to move any room and sophisticated enough for the most discerning audience.
